Teddy Afro

"Music is my life. It is the best way I can express myself and share my thoughts and feelings with the world." - Teddy Afro

Introduction

Tewodros Kassahun, known professionally as Teddy Afro, is an Ethiopian singer-songwriter. He is considered one of the most influential artists in Ethiopia and has gained international recognition for his contributions to Ethiopian music.

Accomplishments

  • Released multiple successful albums including "Yasteseryal", "Tikur Sew" and "Ethiopia"
  • Won numerous awards for his music, including Kora All Africa Music Awards
  • Teddy Afro became the first Ethiopian to become first in billboard in 2017
  • Known for blending various musical styles, including reggae, pop, and traditional Ethiopian music

Discography

  • Abugida: Teddy Afro made his debut in 2001 with the album Abugida, quickly establishing himself as a prominent voice in his native country.
  • Teddy: His second album Teddy was done in 2000 but released in 2002.
  • Yasteseryal: Released in 2005, the album was one of the most influential and controversial. Teddy's lyrical sentiments and the release of this album coincided with the elevated political tension in Ethiopia surrounding the 2005 Ethiopian general election

Awards and Recognitions

  • 2021: Honorary doctorate from Gondar University.
  • 2019: Africa Impact Award in DC
  • 2016: Star of the Generation Award from the city of Winnipeg

Legacy

Teddy Afro's music has played a significant role in shaping the cultural and musical landscape of Ethiopia. His songs often address social and political issues, resonating with people from all walks of life.
